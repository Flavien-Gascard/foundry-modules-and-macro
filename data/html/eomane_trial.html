👁️ EOMANE — Trial of Those Who Would Dream

Overview

Reality folds inward. Sound dulls like deep water. A circular chamber forms around you as the doorway seals silently behind.

"You assemble the mechanism of awakening. Now demonstrate you understand what awakening costs."

▶ Eomane evaluates behavior — not success. This chamber records how future creators behave when reality resists control.

The Chamber

  • 40-ft circular black stone chamber
  • Four memory pedestals
  • Fractured Orrery sigil
  • Floating crystal lens
  • Three abyssal locks
  • Seamless exit

Environmental Behavior

  • Shadows lag behind movement
  • Echoes arrive early
  • Dust falls upward briefly
  • Magic feels watched

Puzzle I — The Steeps Remember

What Players See

Four pedestals glow with memories from your childhood in the Steeps. Sound fades except for distant alley noises.

Pedestal Visions

  • Seymad: bargaining while shielding Aers.
  • Aers: watching danger unseen from shadow.
  • Brijin: stolen knowledge under candlelight.
  • Cedar: dying animal beneath moonlight while life grows onward.

Hidden Rule

▶ A character cannot activate their own pedestal. Another PC must interpret the MEMORY'S MEANING — not events.

Progression

  1. Another PC states meaning.
  2. Owner accepts or allows it.
  3. Pedestal fractures → glyph fragment rises.

Failure Escalation

✗ Fail 1 — Describing events

Memory accelerates.

Rune flashes: "MEANING."

✗ Fail 2 — Rejecting interpretation

Shadow turns toward owner.

"You would rewrite reality to avoid being seen."

✗ Fail 3 — Over-analysis

Lens shows strangers interpreting THEM.

"Another must name you."

The memory dissolves into light. A rune rises and joins the sigil.

Core Mechanic

Two creatures standing on opposite arcs cause their shadows to stretch and connect in a curved bridge of violet light.

▶ Create THREE unique opposite pairings.

Progress

  • Orbit 1 — runes repair
  • Orbit 2 — dust orbits
  • Orbit 3 — lock cracks open

Failures

✗ Adjacent arcs

Energy sputters.

✗ Three participants

Shadow bridges knot and snap.

✗ Repeat pairing

Sigil falls silent.

"Balance through opposition."

Puzzle III — Ravengard's Echo

The crystal lens lowers. Ravengard dies again — differently each time.

Goal

State WHY killing him was necessary AND WHAT IT COST.

Progression

  1. Players speak.
  2. Lens reacts to honesty.
  3. Someone accepts responsibility.

Failures

✗ Pure ambition

Party appears as abyssal tyrants.

✗ "No choice"

Alternate timeline illusion appears.

✗ Silence

Lens becomes a watching eye.

"Say it."

Ravengard looks directly at you… and nods. The image shatters. A lock opens.

Puzzle IV — Brothers' Paradox

Shadow walls separate Seymad and Aers. Whispers tell each opposing truths.

Rule

Perfect agreement fails.

Success Condition

They must declare DIFFERENT but COMPATIBLE truths.

Examples

  • "I will lead." / "Then I will anchor you."
  • "I trust you." / "I doubt you, but remain."

Failures

✗ Perfect unity

Walls tighten.

✗ Hostile betrayal

Lock opens imperfectly (facet corruption).

✗ Indecision

Room begins opening without them.

"Different truths. Same future."

Their shadows finally connect. The lock halves rotate opposite directions. CRACK.
