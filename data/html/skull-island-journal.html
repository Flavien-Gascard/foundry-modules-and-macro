Exploring Skull Island

All location descriptions for Skull Island are keyed to map 24. The island's features are as follows:

  • The island sits in the middle of a vast cavern with a roughly dome-shaped roof that rises to a height of 120 feet above the water level.
  • Atop the island is a stone fortress, its 20-foot-high outer walls interrupted at irregular intervals by 30-foot-high towers topped with battlements, ballistae, and flame cannons.
  • A natural column of rock rises from the heart of the fortress and merges with the cavern ceiling. (See area 9 for more information on this spire.)

SKULL ISLAND WEAPONRY The fortress bristles with ballistae and flame cannons. It takes one action each to load, aim, and fire. Each weapon is AC 15, 50 hp, immune to poison and psychic damage.

Ballista. +6 to hit, range 120/480 ft. Hit: 16 (3d10) piercing damage.
Flame Cannon. +4 to hit, range 60/240 ft. Hit: 3 (1d6) bludgeoning + 17 (5d6) fire damage; target catches fire (1d6 fire/turn, DC 10 Dex to extinguish).

1. Murkspan Bridge

An arched stone bridge connects the island to the town. The middle 30-foot span is rigged to collapse via two stone levers hidden at the south end (DC 20 Perception to find).

2. Main Gate

The main entrance is protected by a sturdy iron gate operated by winches in the flanking guard towers.

3. Harbors

Two natural harbors (3a and 3b), each 60 feet deep with rotted wooden docks. Steel augers along each harbor mouth can be raised to pierce and sink passing ships.

4. Duergar Barracks

Ten duergar are quartered in this two-story building.

5. Human Barracks

This three-story edifice looms over the northeast harbor (area 3b). Seventy human thugs are quartered here.

6. Depleted Armory

The Xanathar Guild plundered this two-story building after taking control of the fortress. It stands empty.

7. Bugbear Barracks

Sixty bugbears inhabit this two-story stone building.

8. Gargoyle Towers

Twelve gargoyles perch on the battlements of these three-story stone towers (four atop each tower).

9. Tower of the Seven Woes

The fortress's half-ogre commander, Sundeth, lives in this hollowed-out spire with his wyvern mount. Sundeth is a hideous 8-foot-tall half-ogre champion with these changes:

  • Sundeth is chaotic evil.
  • He is Large with darkvision 60 ft. and speaks Common and Giant.

The outer door is iron and squeals loudly when opened. Carved into the column is a seven-story prison of windowless torture chambers once used by worshipers of Loviatar.

10. Kuo-toa Lair

East of the fortress, an old dock protrudes from a damp cave like a wooden tongue.

10a. Old Dock

The dock creaks and groans but is safe to stand on.

10b. Kuo-toa Cave

A hungry kuo-toa named Hlool crouches here. Feed it and it will dive to the River Sargauth and return with a bundled cloak of the manta ray as a thank-you gift.
