This level of Undermountain is designed for four 6th-level characters. Those who overcome this level's challenges should amass enough XP to reach 7th level.

What Dwells Here?

A tribe of goblins has claimed many rooms on this level and erected a bazaar. These goblins oppose the Xanathar Guild and its beholder overlord. The rest of the dungeon is infested with sentient magical experiments and leftovers from the days when Halaster's apprentices used this level as a laboratory. The characters might also encounter a troublesome drow, a wererat gang, and members of a doomed adventuring party.

Goblins

The Rustbone tribe of goblins has claimed an old throne room (area 1) and turned it into a bazaar. The Xanathar Guild wants to shut down the market before it attracts too many surface dwellers, while the goblins fight to keep it open. Admission into the goblin bazaar is free, but non-goblinoids are closely watched.

The goblin boss, Yek, recently found a magic circlet that transformed him into a human (see area 1f). After his initial surprise passed, Yek warmed to the transformation. After all, it made him taller. The other goblins quietly resent taking orders from a human and would like to see Yek returned to normal. They might conspire with adventurers toward that end, offering them the circlet as a reward for undoing Yek's "curse."

Xanathar Guild

The Xanathar Guild aims to secure this level, plunder it thoroughly, and return valuable items to Skullport to be sold, thus boosting the town's sagging economy. There are two Xanathar Guild watch posts on the level, and each one has a leader: a drow named Shunn "Spider Eyes" Shurreth commands the northern watch post at area 9, and a human berserker named Nadia the Unbent leads the southern watch post at area 20.

Wererats recently stole a stone key from the southern outpost. Shunn believes that the key unlocks something important (though he doesn't know what that might be) and wants to reclaim it. He intends to capture a wererat and ransom it for the safe return of the key. He offers 50 gp to anyone who brings him a wererat captive or the location of the wererats' hideout (area 14).

Drow and Wererat Gang

A drow mage in league with the Zhentarim has set up a hidden base on this level (area 14). The drow, Rizzeryl, has eight wererats in his employ. The Xanathar Guild is aware of the wererats, but not their drow master.

Rizzeryl has the stone key that the wererats stole from the Xanathar Guild. He thinks it might unlock a Melairkyn vault hidden somewhere on this level, but he's wrong. In fact, the key opens a magic gate that connects levels 6 and 8 of Undermountain. The drow will give up this key in exchange for his life or the destruction of the Xanathar Guild outposts on this level.

Fine Fellows of Daggerford

Characters who explore this level might encounter three members of the Fine Fellows of Daggerford, an evil adventuring party. If Halleth the revenant (see level 1, area 37) accompanies the characters, he uses his Vengeful Tracker trait to lead the party to his former companions so he can repay them for their betrayal. These three "Fine Fellows" are:

Copper Stormforge, a dwarven thief, values gold above all. He has been captured by goblins and imprisoned in area 1e.

Midna Tauberth, a human priest of Shar, thinks she can do everything herself. The perils of this level have forced her to take refuge in area 11b.

Rex the Hammer, a human warrior, seeks fortune and glory, and doesn't care whom he steps on to achieve it. The characters find him at the mercy of the mezzoloth in area 13g.

Mutated Apprentices

Halaster handed over this level to his fervent pupils, almost all of whom were driven mad by their studies and mutated by the magic they wrought, becoming gibbering mouthers and nothics. A mezzoloth summoned from the Lower Planes to serve as a wizard's assistant now rules over them all.

Wandering Monsters

Monsters wander this level of Undermountain in search of food or treasure. Such monsters include carrion crawlers, gricks, mephits, owlbears, and spectators. If your game session needs a greater challenge, try adding one or more of these monsters to an encounter of your own design.

Exploring This Level

1. Goblin Bazaar

In this monstrous market live forty goblins. Half of them are guards, and the other half are traders. The goblin boss and his retinue lurk in area 1e.

1a. Bazaar Side Entrance

Detritus. Trash and debris litter the floor.

Bas-Reliefs. The walls are adorned with bas-reliefs that once depicted dwarves carrying supplies. The dwarves' faces have been chipped away and replaced with cartoonish goblin heads drawn in charcoal.

Noise. Banging noises can be heard to the north. (The goblins in area 1b are building a wooden stage there.)

1b. Auction Hall

The goblins are erecting a stage against the north wall of this 20-foot-high chamber using wood stolen from elsewhere in Undermountain. Once the stage is complete, the goblins plan to auction slaves and other valuable merchandise here. The stage will be quite large when it's finished but is still in the preliminary stages of assembly. A pile of wrecked wooden furniture lies in the area's northwest corner.

Seven goblins are here. Three of them are using stone hammers to pound rusty nails through decrepit wooden planks. The other four are clustered in the middle of the room, passing around a scroll on which someone has drawn a crude blueprint of a stage. If attacked, these goblins retreat to area 1d. They otherwise pay no attention to strangers.

Treasure. The goblins carry a total of 27 cp and 15 sp.

1c. Gibbets and Gravy

Four iron gibbets hang from chains hooked to the 10-foot-high ceiling. The gibbets are empty except for one that holds a wailing goblin named Glom.

Glom was caught stealing food intended for the goblin boss, Yek. She hasn't eaten in two days and happily provides information or her service as a guide in exchange for food or freedom. She knows about the magic circlet that turned Yek into a human and where the goblin boss keeps the rest of his treasure (area 1f).

Glom knows the general layout of this level of Undermountain, the positions of the Xanathar Guild outposts, and the location of the stairs leading down to level 3. Glom eagerly betrays her liberators if someone with more food and the promise of a better life comes along.

The lock on Glom's gibbet can be picked with thieves' tools and a successful DC 15 Dexterity check. It can also be broken off with a successful DC 20 Strength (Athletics) check or a solid weapon blow.

1d. Market Hall

Ceiling. This room has a soaring, 60-foot-high ceiling supported by two rows of stone pillars.

Goblin Vendors. The middle of the chamber is taken up by eight vendor stalls. Twenty-two goblins are here, sixteen vendors (two per stall) and six guards who stand in pairs near the exits to areas 1e, 1f, and 8.

Throne. A granite throne stands against the north wall atop a rectangular stone dais. Yek sits here when he deigns to preside over the market. The throne has been draped with animal skins and festooned with monster skulls and trinkets twisted up in wire.

The goblin vendors buy and sell stolen goods, including items listed in chapter 5 of the Player's Handbook—particularly armor, shields, weapons, adventuring gear, tools, trade goods, food, and drink. The goblins buy goods at half the normal price and sell them at three times the normal price. Their food is of poor quality but edible. A character who succeeds on a DC 12 Charisma (Intimidation or Persuasion) check can bargain a goblin down to half its asking price for an item.

The goblin guards have orders to keep the peace. If a brawl or a battle erupts, they attack with the goal of knocking troublesome visitors unconscious. Visitors whom the guards subdue are dragged out of the market and left in area 8 without their weapons or treasure. (Any treasure the goblins take is placed in area 1f.)

Treasure. The goblin guards have a total of 25 cp and 20 sp. Each goblin vendor carries a fat pouch with 3d6 gp, 3d8 sp, and 3d10 cp in it.

1e. Yek the Tall

Torches. Flickering torches in iron brackets light this room.

Bugbears. Four bugbears stand in the room's corners.

Goblins. A handsome man wearing a golden circlet on his brow (Yek the Tall, a goblin boss in altered form) reclines on a mound of cushions at the north end of the room, eating an apple. Ten goblins (Yek's toadies) lie about on moldy cushions around him.

Prisoner. Chained to the south wall is a male dwarf clad in leather armor who has been gagged. He has copper-orange hair, but his beard has been shaved off (see "Shaved Dwarf" below).

The bugbears work for Yek because he pays them. A bugbear abandons Yek and returns home to Skullport (on level 3) if given a bribe of 5 gp or more.

If Yek orders the goblins into battle, they fight reluctantly. If half are killed or incapacitated, the rest flee. The goblins also run away if Yek is slain.

Yek wears a gold circlet (see "Treasure" below) that has transformed him into a beautiful adult male human. In this form, his size is Medium. If the characters humble themselves before Yek and appeal to his vanity, they are treated fairly. If they attempt to steal from him or have harmed any of his underlings, Yek sentences them to death and orders his goblin toadies and the bugbears to attack. Yek joins the fray only in self-defense.

If the characters and Yek part company on peaceful terms, three of Yek's goblin toadies try to speak to the characters afterward, away from the goblin boss. They plead with the characters to end Yek's "curse" by stealing the circlet. They don't have a plan and are hoping the characters can devise one. The toadies can get all the other goblins out of the hall, but they have no control over Yek's bugbear bodyguards.

Shaved Dwarf
The beardless dwarf is Copper Stormforge, a member of the Fine Fellows of Daggerford adventuring party. His hatred for goblins increased tenfold when they chopped off his beard, and if he is set free, he tries to kill every one of them.

As a reward for setting him free, Copper gives his liberators what he claims is an authentic map of the Sargauth Level (level 3). He doesn't realize the map is fake and utterly worthless.

Copper is a shield dwarf scout (neutral evil). He speaks Common and Dwarvish, has darkvision 60 ft., advantage on saving throws against poison, and resistance to poison damage. He has no weapons.

Treasure. The goblins under Yek carry a total of 60 cp and 25 sp. Each bugbear carries a large pouch containing 15 gp. Yek's circlet is an uncommon magic item called a circlet of human perfection. Only humanoids can attune to it. The circlet transforms its attuned wearer into an attractive human of average height and weight. Removing the circlet ends the effect.

1f. Yek's Treasure

This room contains the following:

  • A gruesome diorama featuring the stuffed corpses of a beholder and six goblins in battle poses, adorned with costume jewelry
  • An ivory chessboard on a low stone table (worth 25 gp)
  • A 9-foot-tall framed painting of Yek in his human form, wearing his golden circlet
  • A hefty, iron-banded wooden chest filled with 2,000 sp

The fifteen pieces of costume jewelry in the diorama are worth 1 gp each. The painting has no real value, but a clever character can sell it for 50 gp with a successful DC 13 Charisma (Deception) check.

2. Kalabash's Chambers

Kalabash was one of Halaster's apprentices, though not one of the original seven. He has been trapped in a pocket dimension for hundreds of years now and is completely insane. Characters might stumble across him as they explore his chambers.

2a. Water Pump

In the center of this room is a rusted iron hand pump and a deep stone basin. This pump draws water from a spring between levels of the dungeon. Using an action to crank the pump draws 1d4 + 1 gallons of potable water into the stone basin.

2b. Abandoned Laboratory

This room is filled with dusty tables and cauldrons. Its stone hearth is cold. Piled atop the tables are alchemical tools and equipment, all of it ancient to the point of being unusable. Disturbing the laboratory in any way alerts the specter in area 2c.

2c. Wizard's Kitchen

This decrepit kitchen contains an oven range, a washing basin, a hearth bearing a covered cook pot, and shelves filled with pots, pans, and cooking tools.

A specter hides in the covered cook pot, emerging if the lid is lifted or if the specter is disturbed by noises from area 2b. The specter can command four nearby pots and pans to do its bidding; treat these objects as flying swords that deal bludgeoning damage instead of slashing damage. If the specter is defeated, the pots and pans clatter to the ground, becoming inanimate once more.

2d. Kalabash's Bedroom

The door to this room bears a sign in Common: "Kalabash's Room. Do not enter unless your name is Halaster."

Furnishings. A dusty bed stands with its headboard against the north wall. At the foot of the bed rests a locked wooden chest (see "Trapped Chest" below). A desk in the southeast corner has stacks of musty old tomes atop it.

Summoning Circle. An old rug woven with arcane symbols lies in the middle of the floor. Beneath the rug, painted on the stone floor, is a 10-foot-diameter summoning circle that has been partially erased.

The circle radiates abjuration magic under detect magic, but three of its activation glyphs have been erased. A character who examines it and succeeds on a DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana or Investigation) check can reconstruct the circle in 1d10 minutes. If completed, it immediately summons Kalabash (CE male Calishite human mage), who attacks any creatures he sees. He is poisoned until he sobers up or a lesser restoration spell is cast on him. Erasing part of the completed circle returns him to his pocket dimension.

Trapped Chest
The wooden chest is of solid construction, and its steel lock is warded with a magic trap. Picking the lock requires thieves' tools and a successful DC 15 Dexterity check. If that check does not succeed, or if anyone tries to break or smash the lock, the mechanism releases a small bolt of lightning that deals 10 (3d6) lightning damage to the creature tampering with it (no saving throw). The chest is empty except for a note: "Retirement fund. Don't spend this on ale, Kalabash!"

Treasure. The desk has twenty old blank books on it. Each can be sold for 10 gp.

3. Halaster Puppet

Both doors to this room are difficult to open because furniture inside has been piled against them (DC 15 Strength Athletics). The room contains the following:

Automaton. An animated, life-size puppet of Halaster Blackcloak is hard at work on an experiment. The puppet is made primarily of wood and canvas, with a shoddy gray cloak draped over its shoulders. Dozens of cloth eyes have been sewn into the cloak.

Table. The puppet looms over a stone table covered with dusty alchemical equipment and vials of various substances—nothing valuable or dangerous.

The puppet responds to any movement within 20 feet and threatens any creature that comes closer, shouting in Halaster's voice: "Terrible and painful magical death! Fireballs and lightning bolts! Begone!" It speaks and understands Common but voices nothing but empty threats. If asked what it's working on, it shouts, "Magic!" and chuckles. A character who watches it and succeeds on a DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) check ascertains that the puppet isn't doing anything meaningful.

The puppet is a Medium construct with AC 10, 8 hit points, speed 20 ft., immunity to poison and psychic damage, and ability scores of 10. It can't be charmed or frightened and is not subject to exhaustion. It has no attacks and falls to pieces if reduced to 0 hit points or hit by a successful dispel magic (DC 15). Hidden inside is a flask of alchemist's fire that shatters as the puppet falls apart—its remnants catch fire but harm no nearby creatures.

If the puppet is destroyed, Halaster's voice calls out: "Auugh! Now who will find the cure to filth fever?!"

4. Abandoned Camp

The Xanathar Guild had a base camp here, but it was forced to abandon the place after a run-in with the Fine Fellows of Daggerford.

This room is strewn with rotten supplies and the remains of a fire pit. Three bugbear corpses and an intellect devourer's body are heaped in the northeast corner. Searching the corpses reveals a scrap of parchment with the following phrase written in Common: "As many as there are stars in the sky." This is the pass phrase to safely enter the Xanathar Guild outpost at area 9.

5. Arch Gate to Level 4

This dusty room is hidden behind a secret door disguised to look like a plain section of wall.

Embedded in the middle of the room's north wall is a stone arch, its frame engraved with images of falling coins. The arch's keystone has a tiny slot in it. This is one of Halaster's magic gates, and its rules are as follows:

  • Feeding a gold coin into the slot causes the coin to disappear and the gate to open for 1 minute. Other coins disappear but don't open the gate.
  • Characters must be 8th level or higher to pass through this gate. The first creature to pass through triggers an elder rune.
  • A creature that passes through appears in area 11c on level 4, in the closest unoccupied space next to the identical gate there.

6. Music of the Dead

The hallway leading to this chamber is filled with the haunting sound of a harpsichord playing.

Harpsichord. In the middle of the room is a harpsichord made entirely of bones—human, dwarf, ogre, and halfling finger bones for keys. Somber music emanates from it. The harpsichord stops when a living creature comes within 10 feet of it.

Carving. Carved into the north wall is a single measure of musical notes. They are faded and difficult to read.

A detect magic spell reveals an aura of conjuration magic around the harpsichord and a smaller aura of necromancy magic emanating from something inside it. Any character proficient with a musical instrument can correctly play the displayed notes. Any other character who tries must make a DC 20 Intelligence (Performance) check. Success causes a decorative skull above the keys to fall away, revealing the treasure. On a failed check, the performer must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. Failure deals 22 (4d10) necrotic damage and leaves the performer with bleached white skeletal hands (functional, but unsettling).

Treasure. Hidden inside the harpsichord is a spell scroll of raise dead. Smashing the harpsichord open damages the scroll and renders it useless.

7. Shocking Discoveries

Several chambers where mining equipment and copper ore were once stored are now occupied by laboratories devoted to experiments in electricity and necromancy. Trenzia, one of Halaster's new apprentices, conducts her research here.

7a. Trenzia's Workroom

This well-maintained workroom reeks of rotting flesh and ozone. Odd metal instruments for measuring electricity rest atop tables, along with scraps of parchment noting "rate of decay" and "accumulation of charge."

Log Entry. A thorough search yields a scrap from Trenzia's log: "Day 1: Halaster has granted me this abandoned copper mine in the Arcane Chambers. Finally, I have access to the components needed for my experiments." Everything beyond that point is smudged and illegible.

7b. Ghoul Barrels

Barrels. Twelve sealed wooden barrels covered in dust rest against the walls. Three are empty, three contain fist-sized chunks of copper ore (50 gp worth per barrel), and the remaining six each have one ghoul crammed inside. Disturbing any of these barrels causes all six ghouls to burst forth and attack.

Hammock. A hammock laden with blankets hangs from anchors in the north wall.

Copper-Plated Door. A copper-plated door is set into the southeast corner. It is connected to the lightning grid in area 7c. Unless the creatures in area 7c have been destroyed, the door emits a soft hum when approached. A creature that touches the humming door must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw, taking 10 (3d6) lightning damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. A creature wearing metal armor has disadvantage on the saving throw.

Log Entry. Tucked into the hammock's blankets is another page from Trenzia's log: "Day 3. I no longer trust the other apprentices. What they see as mysticism I know to be science! I can't stand their incantations any longer. If any of them try to intrude on my laboratory again, they're in for a truly shocking surprise!"

7c. Trenzia's Laboratory

Trenzia turned this laboratory into a giant lightning trap, sacrificing herself in its construction.

Copper Tiles. The floor is plated with copper tiles.

Lightning Skull. The metal-coated skull of a half-elf (Trenzia) floats around the room, giving off crackling sounds as it electrifies the floor with rays of lightning (see "Trenzia" below).

Golem. A flesh golem stands in the middle of the room amid destroyed tables, shards of broken glass, and rusty mining equipment.

Chute. A 5-foot-wide, 5-foot-high chute in the southeast corner curves downward, descending 20 feet to area 13a. No ability check is required to descend it.

Trenzia (Modified Flameskull)
Trenzia convinced Halaster to transform her into a flameskull, with these changes: resistance to fire damage; immunity to lightning damage; lightning bolt prepared instead of fireball; Fire Ray replaced with Lightning Ray (lightning damage).

On each of its turns, the skull uses its action to electrify the copper-plated floor. The floor remains electrified until the start of the skull's next turn. Any creature that steps onto or starts its turn on the copper tiles takes 10 (3d6) lightning damage. Instead of taking damage, the flesh golem regains 10 hit points whenever it starts its turn on the electrified floor.

If the skull is destroyed and later rejuvenates, it resumes electrifying the floor.

The flesh golem has instructions to kill all intruders and never leave the room. If it goes berserk and attacks the skull, the skull uses its next action to try to calm the golem—while doing so, it cannot electrify the floor.

7d. Mining Woes

Three ghasts hunker down behind the door leading into this room, waiting to attack.

This room contains the remains of a mining operation. Pickaxes and hammers litter the floor, as do the gnawed bones, torn black robes, and rubber boots of a female half-elf wizard. Notably absent is the half-elf's skull. (The bones and clothing belonged to Trenzia. Halaster fed her body to the ghasts.)

7e. Copper Vein

Copper Vein. An exposed bit of stonework has a thick vein of copper ore running through it.

Log Entry. A scrap of paper on the floor reads: "Day 10. With lightning and copper wires, I created a pack of ghouls. Hal was not impressed. He says I'm not ready for Dweomercore and its arcane secrets. I'll show him. His flesh golem has given me an idea for a trap more devious than any Hal could devise. Lightning—is there anything it can't do?"

8. Fresco Cross Hall

Frescoes on the walls of this four-way intersection depict shield dwarves at work. Magic causes the images to move. The dwarves roll and stack barrels, carve blocks of stone, drink from stone tankards, and so forth. As the characters make their way down each hall, the dwarves wave at them and occasionally wink. These animated frescoes are harmless.

9. Spider Eyes Watch Post

The Xanathar Guild claims this corner of the dungeon. Shunn Shurreth, a drow exile favored by the beholder crime lord, commands the forces stationed here.

9a. Trapped Hall

Bugbears. Two bugbears stand guard at the east end of this long hall, where it narrows to a width of 10 feet.

Dart Traps. The northern wall conceals five poison dart traps, triggered by hidden pressure plates in the floor.

The bugbears, Bolgus and Bulkar, disarm the traps for Xanathar Guild members. When unfamiliar creatures approach from the west, Bolgus shouts for them to halt and Bulkar asks, "How many eyes does Xanathar have?" The correct reply is, "As many as there are stars in the sky." If anyone gives the wrong pass phrase, the bugbears invite them forward but don't disarm the traps.

Poison Darts. Each trap shoots four darts (+8 to hit) at a random target above the pressure plate (triggered by 20+ lbs of weight). On a hit: 2 (1d4) piercing damage, plus DC 15 Constitution save or 10 (3d6) poison damage. Each trap fires three times before its supply is exhausted.

Hidden Lever. A lever concealed by a false section of stone near the bugbears arms and disarms the traps. DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) to find.

9b. Shunn's Gang

Lanterns. Battered oil lanterns rest atop toppled blocks of stone, illuminating this partially collapsed hall.

Furnishings. Crates, barrels, and seven wooden cots line the walls.

Xanathar Guild. Seven Xanathar Guild members (NE human thugs) and their leader, Shunn "Spider Eyes" Shurreth, gather around a campfire in the middle of the hall. Two intellect devourers have taken residence in the skulls of two guild members and use their Detect Sentience trait to warn of intruders.

Shunn Shurreth
A drow priest of Lolth cursed Shunn and forced him into exile. The curse partially transformed him into a spider, giving him eight red arachnid eyes, fangs, and black bristles on his limbs. A remove curse or greater restoration would restore his form, but Shunn refuses—his horrific appearance gives him influence over his gang and favor with Xanathar.

Shunn is a drow elite warrior (lawful evil) who can climb any surface without ability checks while cursed. He prefers diplomacy and offers 50 gp to characters who find the wererats troubling his gang and return the stone key they stole.

If attacked, Shunn calls for bugbear reinforcements from area 9d. His goal is to capture enemies and haul them to Skullport.

Supplies. The barrels and crates contain enough water and dry food for a single person for 600 days (or Shunn's gang for 30 days).

Treasure. The thugs carry a total of 14 gp. Around his neck, Shunn wears a silver key (5 gp) that unlocks the silver-bound chest in area 9c.

9c. Gang's Treasure

Barrels. Stone racks spanning the walls contain barrels so rotted they fall apart when handled.

Table and Chest. A large stone table in the middle of the room is draped with cobwebs. A silver-bound wooden chest rests atop it, unlocked by Shunn's silver key (also DC 15 Dexterity with thieves' tools). The chest contains 100 gp.

9d. Bugbear Barracks

Ten bugbears sleep on the dusty floor of this room. They investigate any commotion they hear in area 9b.

10. Ooze Temple

These four rooms comprise a temple devoted to water and oozes. All doors leading to these rooms are decorated with symbols of water, and each door forms an airtight seal when shut.

10a. Skull in the Cube

Clean. This 30-foot-high room is swept clean, with no trace of dust or debris.

Frescoes. The walls are carved with frescoes depicting horrific cyclopean cities of black stone submerged beneath raging seas.

Giant Ooze. A gelatinous cube measuring 30 feet on a side is trapped in the room. Floating in its center is a dwarven skull sheathed in glass. A DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check reveals that the skull is suspended in an enormous gelatinous cube, not levitating.

The skull (which belonged to a duergar) radiates transmutation magic. It preserves the duergar's magical ability to enlarge itself, causing the gelatinous cube to become Gargantuan. Removing the skull shrinks the cube to its normal size (10 feet on a side). While enlarged, the cube can hold up to nine Large or thirty-six Medium/smaller creatures, can make four Pseudopod attacks, and makes Strength checks and saves with advantage.

10b. Flooded Room

This laboratory (20-foot-high ceiling) is completely filled with 24,000 cubic feet of salt water created by a spell gone awry. Opening the door causes the water to empty into area 10a. Creatures within 20 feet of the opened door must make a DC 12 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone and take 5 (1d10) bludgeoning damage.

Swept in with the water is the putrid, rotting corpse of Salamanth, one of Halaster's more foolish apprentices, clad in green and purple robes.

Treasure. A search reveals overturned tables, waterlogged books, and a glass wand (arcane focus) worth 25 gp.

10c. Altar to Juiblex

False Altar. In the middle of this 20-foot-high room is a limestone altar topped with a misshapen mound of gold—actually an ochre jelly of unusual color trapped in magical stasis.

Wall Carvings. Black mildew drips from lidless eyes and gaping mouths carved into the walls, representing the demon lord Juiblex.

The altar bears an inscription in Abyssal: "A gift of flesh, willingly given, can feed our eternal hunger." The stasis field immobilizes the ooze and renders it impervious to damage. It is dispelled when it comes into contact with flesh or is targeted by a successful dispel magic (DC 17). Freed from stasis, the ochre jelly attacks.

10d. Caved-In Room

This room has partially caved in, but someone has bored tunnels through the collapsed sections, granting access to dungeon hallways south and east. The floor is covered with dust and debris but contains nothing of interest.

11. Midna's Lair

An apprentice of Halaster's claimed this area as her own work space centuries ago, using her mastery of the unseen servant spell to create multiple invisible helpers. Even after her death, her unseen servants persisted, waiting for her to return. Midna Tauberth, one of the Fine Fellows of Daggerford, recently intruded upon this hall and is using it as a rest area.

11a. Hall of Degeneration

The walls of this passage are painted with images of wizards succumbing to madness and being transformed into nothics. One particularly vivid image shows shafts of eerie green light exploding out of a spellbook and changing a wizard into a gibbering mouther.

11b. Master of the Unseen

Flickering torchlight created by continual flame spells spills out of this room into the hall. The 15-foot-high vaulted chamber contains the following:

Light. Freestanding iron torch sconces in the four corners have continual flame spells cast upon them.

Midna. Midna Tauberth (NE female human priest of Shar) relaxes in one of several overstuffed chairs, waited on by nine living unseen servants. Around her neck she wears two holy symbols: one shaped like a gold coin (Waukeen), the other a black disk outlined in purple (Shar).

Furnishings. Empty bookshelves and desks line the walls. Against the south wall stands a long banquet table bearing fresh foodstuffs on copper platters and flagons filled with wine. A portrait of a smiling Halaster hangs above the table. Each day at dawn, the platters are mystically replenished with enough food and wine for twelve people—so long as the table remains in this room.

Midna isn't interested in sharing her lair. She orders her servants to beat intruders who don't leave. If the servants seem to be losing, she calls them off and apologizes, allowing the newcomers to partake of the food and wine.

If Halleth the revenant is present, he tries to kill Midna and reclaim his holy symbol of Waukeen, which she stole from his corpse. If Midna is slain and the holy symbol returned to Halleth, the revenant's thirst for vengeance abates and both he and the holy symbol turn to dust.

Living Unseen Servants. The servants obey Midna because she reminds them of their creator. When Midna utters a command, all servants follow it—she cannot issue orders to a single servant.

Treasure. Midna's holy symbol of Shar is a small obsidian disk on a silver chain (25 gp). The holy symbol of Waukeen is a small gold disk stamped with the god's visage (25 gp). The smiling portrait of Halaster is worth 25 gp but awkward to transport.

12. Dwarven Tools

These hallways are lined with crumbling stone tables and shelves upon which rest sturdy dwarven anvils, whetstones, tongs, hammers, chisels, and other tools.

Arch Gate to Level 5
Embedded in the wall at the end of the westward hallway is an arch gate. Close inspection reveals a dead tree carved into its keystone. Its rules:
  • Touching the arch with a dead twig or branch causes the gate to open for 1 minute.
  • Characters must be 8th level or higher to pass through. The first creature triggers an elder rune.
  • A creature that passes through appears in area 3b on level 5.
Level 5 area 3b description: Each door of this guardhouse is barricaded shut from within (DC 15 Strength Athletics). Inside: a stone arch in the east wall (the return gate), a wooden trestle table lying on its side, an iron chandelier above it, and a dead halfling in leather armor tucked behind an iron stove in the northeast corner, his face twisted into a mask of horror.

13. Mutated Apprentices

Once a dwarven mithral mine, this section of the dungeon is home to apprentices whose attempts to unlock the secrets of the Weave transformed them into gibbering mouthers and nothics.

13a. Dumathoin's Altar

Gibbering Mouther. A gibbering mouther wanders the hall to the east, attacking other creatures on sight.

Equipment. Overturned mine carts and discarded mining equipment are scattered across the floor.

Altar. A red stone altar covered with small, dirty handprints stands in the northern alcove. It is a 3-foot-tall, 500-pound tapered block of dark red stone engraved with Dwarvish prayers to Dumathoin, the Keeper of Secrets under the Mountain. Greasy goblin handprints cover the altar—a desecration any dwarf would find offensive. A character who takes the time to clean the altar gains a magical boon: all secret doors are plainly visible for the next 24 hours. Willfully desecrating the altar beforehand nullifies this boon.

Treasure. Beneath one of the overturned mine carts are four chunks of mithral ore, each worth 25 gp and weighing 10 pounds.

13b. Wall of Rust and Bones

Bisecting this room is an 8-foot-high, 2-foot-thick wall of hobgoblin skeletons and rusty armor, with a 2-foot gap between the top of the wall and the ceiling. A Tiny creature can pass through narrow openings. Climbing over requires a DC 12 Strength (Athletics) check. The wall can be broken apart or pushed down, but doing so creates a lot of clatter and summons the nothics from area 13e.

13c–13d. Vacant Washrooms

Inside each of these rooms is a rusted water pump (inoperable), basins, and stone toilets separated by thin slate dividers.

13e. "Nothic to See Here!"

An arcane lock spell has been cast on the western door. Rizzeryl the drow mage (see area 14) can pass through freely. Other creatures must force the door open with a successful DC 25 Strength (Athletics) check.

Nothics. Four nothics lurk here. They are insane and attack most creatures they encounter. They are quickly cowed by displays of magic, however. Once they realize they're in the presence of spellcasters, the nothics become slightly more cooperative. They know that several bipedal rat creatures are holed up on the other side of the western door, and that their master is a "purple-skinned elf wizard with red eyes and white hair."

Equipment. Low-lying stone shelves along the walls are stuffed with crumbling pairs of dwarven boots. Rusty dwarven helmets and dusty goggles with tattered leather straps hang on pegs above them.

13f. Gibbering Mine

The walls in this partially collapsed section of the dungeon are streaked with veins of copper. Three gibbering mouthers have gathered here and begin to jabber as other creatures approach. They fight until killed.

13g. Strike it Rich

The characters hear sounds of battle as they traverse the hall leading to this 10-foot-high room.

Light. The room is lit by a single lantern resting in the middle of the floor.

Equipment. Mine carts about the room contain glittering heaps of copper ore.

Battle. A wounded warrior named Rex the Hammer (LE male Illuskan human champion, 22 hit points remaining) has been backed into the southeast corner by a vicious mezzoloth and two nothics. Three dead nothics killed by Rex lie on the floor.

The mezzoloth was summoned and bound to Undermountain by Halaster to prevent looting. It uses telepathy to command the nothics, which serve it out of fear.

Rex the Hammer
Rex was the founder and leader of the Fine Fellows of Daggerford. Assuming his group would run roughshod over the monsters of Undermountain, he struck off on his own after growing fed up with his companions' bickering—and immediately got ambushed.

If the characters rescue him, Rex expresses gratitude and quaffs his healing potion. He seems amiable, confident, and eager to explore. He's happy to join for an equal share of loot. Once at full health, though, he sheds his civil veneer and begins bullying the party, berating and threatening anyone who defies him. The bullying escalates until he is forcibly ousted or the party dissolves.
Halleth & Rex — Banter
Rex: (nonchalantly) Well, well, if it isn't the ghost of Halleth. Come to haunt me?
Halleth: (cold and angry) Haunt you? No, Rex. I've come for something far more satisfying — revenge.
Rex: Revenge? Oh, please. You knew the risks. Sometimes weak links need to be severed.
Halleth: Weak link? You betrayed me, Rex! I trusted you. We were supposed to watch each other's backs, not plunge a sword through them!
Rex: Trust? That's a luxury for the foolish. I did what I had to do for power. You were holding me back.
Halleth: (voice echoing with resentment) You think killing me granted you power? You're a fool. All you gained was the ire of the afterlife.
Rex: If you're here to lecture me, save your breath. I don't regret a thing.
Halleth: Your conscience may be buried beneath your arrogance, but it will claw its way to the surface.
Rex: Conscience? I left that behind long ago. I've embraced the darkness. You can't touch me.
Halleth: Darkness may be your ally, but it's also your undoing. Every wicked deed has consequences — and I am yours.
Rex: (laughs mockingly) Come then. I'll send you back to the afterlife once and for all.
Halleth: Your time will come, Rex. I am but the beginning of your reckoning.

Treasure. Rex's backpack contains a silvered shortsword, 30 feet of hempen rope, 2 days of rations, a flask of oil, a pouch with 13 gp, and a potion of healing. The oil lantern in the middle of the room also belongs to Rex. The mine carts contain over a thousand chunks of copper ore (10 lbs, 1 gp each); the carts themselves are too rusted to move.

14. Base de Résistance

These two chambers have been claimed by a drow mage named Rizzeryl, his summoned quasit minion, and eight wererats. Rizzeryl works for both House Auvryndar (a low-ranking drow house from Menzoberranzan slowly consolidating power in Undermountain) and the Zhentarim (which is trying to drive the Xanathar Guild out of Skullport). Rizzeryl and the wererats have no quarrel with adventurers who leave them alone.

14a. Rizzeryl's Hideout

The door to this room bears a mural of a darkmantle descending on an unsuspecting dwarf. An arcane lock spell seals the door; Rizzeryl and his wererats pass freely, while others need a successful DC 25 Strength check (or magic) to force it open.

Drow. Rizzeryl sits in the southwest corner while resting in a trance. If he hears someone approach through the eastern door, he casts greater invisibility on himself.

Table. A wooden table in the center bears a map made of loose stones arranged to form the walls and doors of this level. The map omits chambers hidden behind secret doors (areas 5, 20c, 20d, 22e, 22f, 25h, and 26b).

Rizzeryl prefers to be invisible while engaging intruders in conversation, learning their intentions before revealing himself. He offers a reward for the destruction of both Xanathar Guild outposts (areas 9 and 20) and the heads of both guild leaders, Nadia the Unbent and Shunn Shurreth. Delivering the heads earns a stone key from the secret compartment below. If attacked, Rizzeryl calls forth the wererats while summoning a quasit. He will trade the stone key for his life if overpowered.

Secret Compartment
Beneath Rizzeryl's chair is a loose floor tile concealing a hidden compartment (DC 15 Wisdom Perception). It contains: Rizzeryl's leather-bound spellbook (all prepared spells, plus arcane lock and sending); a 12-inch-long stone key; and a 12-inch-long driftwood wand carved with Xanathar's symbol (a circle with ten equidistant spokes). The wand is nonmagical and lets Rizzeryl slip past the guardian in area 20a and move through Skullport unmolested.

The stone key weighs 10 pounds and bears the inscription in Dwarvish: "Meet me on the Lost Level. Find my twin in Slitherswamp." It opens a magic gate connecting levels 6 and 8 of Undermountain.

14b. Wererat Barracks

Eight wererats in human form sleep on dirty cots in this room. They are loyal to the Zhentarim and determined to push the Xanathar Guild out of Skullport and Undermountain. Their leader is Zilitsa Ilvarren, a crass, middle-aged woman who has a good rapport with Rizzeryl. The others are Frek, Henk, Kozrik, Arreth, Elabbor, Kreena, and Sybilee.

Treasure. An unlocked wooden chest contains 30 gp, a copper tankard similar to those in area 22a (5 gp), a battered silver scepter topped with a Dwarvish rune meaning "justice" (25 gp), and three worthless chapbooks titled Out of the Inferno, Vols. 1, 2, and 3.

15. Dusty Throne

Ruins. Much of this room collapsed long ago, and what remains is covered with dust.

Footprints. Footprints (left by wererats in human form) crisscross the dusty floor.

Throne. A plain stone throne stands against the south wall. Beyond it, heaps of rubble fill a collapsed tunnel. A search of the throne and surrounding room yields nothing of value—the wererats in area 14 have already been here.

16. Partially Collapsed Room

The back of this room has collapsed, filling the chamber with dust and debris. No one has been here in ages, and nothing of value remains.

17. Hungry Rust Monsters

This old festival hall has an arched ceiling 30 feet high.

Rust Monsters. Two rust monsters are fighting over a rusted steel helmet in the middle of this room, casting it about as they knock each other around.

Wall Decor. The walls are decorated with bas-reliefs of dwarves singing and drinking. A dozen empty iron torch brackets are mounted to the north and south walls at 10-foot intervals.

The helm provides a momentary distraction that characters can use to cross the hall safely on their first visit. The next time they come here, the rust monsters are waiting. Tearing loose a torch bracket requires an action and a DC 15 Strength (Athletics) check; one bracket distracts the rust monsters for 1d6 minutes.

18. Cold Storage

The Melairkyn dwarves used this chamber for cold storage. Halaster has turned it into a trap.

Cold. The room is bitterly cold (0 degrees Fahrenheit).

Glowing Rune. The walls rise 10 feet, then angle inward to form a pyramidal roof with a 40-foot-high apex. Inscribed on the sloped north wall is a 10-foot-tall Dwarvish rune that glows with a faint blue light—an ancient symbol meaning "cold," radiating conjuration magic.

When a spell is cast in this room (including from a magic item), the giant rune flashes and deals 36 (8d8) cold damage to all creatures in the room. Once triggered, the rune cannot do so again for 1 hour.

19. Giant Spider Den

Giant spiders make their home here despite countless attempts by adventurers to get rid of them.

19a. Arachnid Down

Clutter. Narrow paths wind between scattered piles of broken furniture and garbage.

Chandeliers. Two iron chandeliers, once anchored to the 20-foot-high ceiling, have fallen—their ropes cut. One has crashed into a pile of debris. Pinned beneath the other is a dead giant spider.

19b. Old Bedchamber

Dead Spider. A dead giant spider, shot full of arrows and scorched by fire, lies next to a broken wooden bed against the east wall.

Armoire. Standing against the south wall is a wooden armoire draped in cobwebs.

Treasure. The armoire contains the skeletal remains of a halfling adventurer who was trapped inside by giant spiders and starved to death. The skeleton is clad in a chain shirt and wears a ring of swimming on one bony finger. Also present: an explorer's pack (minus water and rations).

19c. Sticky Webs

This room is full of thick, sticky webs (see "Dungeon Hazards" in DMG chapter 5). The webbing stretches all the way to areas 19d and 19e. Any tugging or burning of the webs alerts the giant spiders in area 19d.

19d. Spider Warren

Five giant spiders lair here and attack all intruders.

19e. Spider Larder

Dense webbing fills this room as well. Suspended within the webs are six cocoons. Five contain the desiccated corpses of four goblins and a nothic. The sixth contains a swarm of insects (spiders) that bursts forth and attacks if the cocoon is torn open.

Treasure. The dead nothic has no treasure. A thorough search of the goblin corpses yields 20 cp, 18 sp, 5 gp, and a silvered dagger.

20. Dead Eyes Watch Post

The Xanathar Guild forces stationed here include five human thugs, eight bugbears, and their leader, Nadia the Unbent. A beholder zombie provides added security.

20a. Beholder Zombie

Watcher. A beholder zombie floats near the 20-foot-high ceiling, out of melee range. Painted in white around its central eye is Xanathar's symbol (a circle with ten equidistant spokes).

Noise. Sounds of mock battle emanate from the tunnel to the west (leading to area 20b).

The beholder zombie attacks anyone who enters without brandishing or wearing the symbol of Xanathar. Combat in this room brings reinforcements from area 20b.

20b. Watch Post Barracks

Xanathar Guild. Unless drawn to area 20a by sounds of combat: Nadia the Unbent (NE female Illuskan berserker, 7 feet tall with shaggy black hair and pale skin), five human thugs, and five bugbears are training here.

Blood Circle. Painted in blood on the middle of the floor is a 10-foot-diameter Xanathar symbol. Nadia stands in the middle of it, armed with a 10-foot pole, knocking her underlings about in a contest to see who can push her out of the circle.

Hammocks. Fifteen leather hammocks are hooked to stone brackets at regular intervals around the walls.

Secret Door. A secret door in the west wall leads to area 20c.

Nadia relishes any opportunity to fight and kill, hurling herself and her forces into battle without a second thought.

20c. Arch Gate to Level 6

This hallway has three alcoves. The two southernmost contain secret doors to areas 20b and 20d. The alcove to the north has an arch embedded in its back wall, its keystone carved with an image of a rust monster.

Arch Gate. Rules for this gate:

  • Touching the arch with a nonmagical item made entirely of ferrous metal (such as an iron spike) reduces the item to powdered rust and opens the gate for 1 minute.
  • Characters must be 9th level or higher to pass through. The first creature triggers an elder rune.
  • A creature that passes through appears in area 24 on level 6.

20d. Secret Armory

This 20-foot-high room is hidden behind a secret door.

Bugbears. Three bugbears have broken away from the main group to search this room for treasure. They have worked their way to the east end of the room and aren't expecting trouble.

Racks. Weapon and armor racks stand like library bookshelves in the middle of the room, with dusty, web-filled aisles between them.

Sharpening Wheel. Half embedded in the floor in the middle of the room is a 1-foot-thick, 6-foot-diameter stone sharpening wheel. A stone pedal causes it to turn when stepped on.

The room contains dozens of suits of plate armor, scale armor, and chain mail, as well as scores of shields, battleaxes, morningstars, warhammers, war picks, and javelins. Most have deteriorated to the point of being useless—rust has eaten away the metal, and dampness has rotted the leather straps and bindings.

21. Animated Ballistae

Light. The arched, 30-foot-high ceiling of this long hall is studded with glowing red crystals that cast dim light throughout. Crystals pried out of their fixtures go dark.

Animated Ballistae. Parked in front of the door to area 22a are two animated ballistae. Each is a Large wood-and-iron construct (AC 15, 50 hp, speed 30 ft., immunity to poison) that fires magic bolts of fire (+6 to hit, 16 (3d10) fire damage). They have blindsight 120 feet and cannot attack creatures directly above them. Challenge rating 2 (450 XP) each.

The ballistae ignore creatures that keep to the easternmost 60-foot section of the hallway. Once they detect intruders, they advance. If a ballista has nothing to attack on its turn, it returns to its starting position.

22. Garrux's Brewery

This complex was built by a dwarf named Garrux, who served as Master of Ale and Provisions for the Melairkyn dwarves.

22a. Ale Dispenser

Pillar. A stone pillar in the middle of the room has a ring of six copper spigots jutting out of it, 3 feet above the floor. These pipes connect to the vats in area 22c.

Bas-Reliefs. Carved into the walls are twenty bas-reliefs depicting drunken dwarves in different poses. One hand of each dwarf protrudes from the wall; three grasp copper tankards.

Nothing happens if five of the six spigots are turned. Turning the sixth causes green slime to pour out of it.

Treasure. Three copper tankards remain on the wall carvings (most have vanished over the years; one is in area 14b). Each is worth 5 gp.

22b. "Brewers Only!"

A 10-foot-high, 10-foot-square room nestled between areas 22a and 22c. A brass plaque on the western door reads in Dwarvish: "BREWERS ONLY! Others keep out! By order of Garrux, Master of Ale and Provisions."

22c. Ale Vats

Vats. Six stone vats, arranged in two rows of three, dominate this 20-foot-high room. Each is a hollow cylinder 10 feet high and 10 feet in diameter with 6-inch-thick walls, a copper spigot at 3 feet, and a narrow stone staircase curling to a hinged copper lid.

Doors and Barrels. A double door in the west wall has stacks of old barrels on both sides.

Four of the vats are empty. The southwest vat contains a beholder zombie, which rises out of the vat and attacks if disturbed.

Barrels. Thirty empty iron-banded zurkhwood barrels line the walls. Most can no longer hold liquids without leaking, but clever characters can use them to safely cross the pit traps in area 22d.

22d. Barrel Run

Beyond a 10-foot landing, this hall gradually slopes down toward the west. Secret doors at the west end open into areas 22e and 22f. Dwarves used to fill barrels of ale and roll them down this hall to be stored in the secret chambers.

Pit Traps. Three 20-foot-deep pits are built into the sloped floor. Each pit cover blends with the surrounding floor (DC 15 Wisdom Perception to spot). Weight of 50+ pounds opens the trapdoor, causing the creature to fall (2d6 bludgeoning damage). The lid snaps closed after. A DC 20 Strength check is needed to pry it open from the outside; DC 15 Dexterity (thieves' tools) from the inside.

Wedging four or more iron spikes under a pit lid prevents it from opening. The lids won't open if an object (such as a barrel) is merely rolled over them. A character can circumvent all traps by climbing into a barrel and rolling down the hall, or by balancing atop a rolling barrel (DC 20 Dexterity Acrobatics).

22e. North Ale Storage

This chamber is hidden behind a secret door. The room's walls climb 10 feet, then angle inward to form a pyramidal cap with a 30-foot-high apex. Stacked in the middle are 140 zurkhwood barrels (banded in iron), forming a 21-foot-high pyramid with a base seven barrels wide. Each lid bears a magical glyph (abjuration aura).

Treasure. Each barrel contains 40 gallons of magically preserved dwarven ale. Removing or breaking the lid ends the preservation spell. Sold as "Melairkyn Ale" or "Undermountain Ale," a barrel fetches 40 gp in Waterdeep. A full barrel weighs approximately 400 pounds.

22f. South Ale Storage

This room is identical to area 22e, except the pyramid of barrels has collapsed. Most lie smashed on the floor. Fifty-nine remain intact, their ale preserved. Three more appear intact but are actually three mimics placed by Halaster—they lurk within 10 feet of one another and fight together.

23. Ruined Dwarven Temple

These rooms once formed a temple dedicated to Dumathoin, the Keeper of Secrets under the Mountain, but Halaster has destroyed and replaced most of their original contents.

23a. Defaced Dwarves

Light. The walls climb 15 feet, then angle inward to create a peaked, 30-foot-high ceiling. The sloped upper walls have red glowing crystals set into them (dim light; crystals go dark if pried out).

Carvings. The lower walls were once lined with carvings depicting dwarves pushing mine carts westward, but these have been defaced and replaced with stony tentacles that protrude from the walls. The tentacles writhe as creatures pass—a harmless effect created by the Mad Mage.

Dwarf-Guarded Doors. A 20-foot-tall double door stands closed at the west end of the hall. Carved into the walls flanking the double door are armored dwarves, their stony beards flowing from great helms.

23b. Hall of Dead Dragons

Ceiling. The 30-foot-high vaulted ceiling of this room is supported every 20 feet or so by thick stone arches.

Skeletal Displays. Standing in the hall are the skeletons of four Huge dragons, their bones held together by wire and cement. A fifth skeleton in similar disrepair fills an alcove to the south.

The hall contains posed, inanimate skeletons of five adult dragons—one each of brass, bronze, copper, gold, and silver. The gold dragon skeleton occupies the southern alcove. The skeletons are harmless.

24. Dead Adventurer

What was once a dwarven shrine is now the tomb of a long-dead adventurer.

Splayed across a broken stone bench in the middle of the room are the skeletal remains of a tiefling in rotted leather armor, clutching a quarterstaff that has become brittle with age. A tattered backpack contains adventuring gear that has rotted or rusted away.

Casting speak with dead on the skeleton reveals that the tiefling, Savir, was a monk who fell prey to a cloaker. Savir's spirit doesn't know what became of his companions or the cloaker. If questioned about Undermountain, the spirit reveals that there's an elven tomb nearby with a secret door in the north wall that neither he nor his companions could open (area 26a).

25. Creature Storage

Halaster uses these chambers as a storage facility for creatures he has captured and petrified. He restores them to flesh using the elven magic in area 25a.

25a. Caryatids of Restoration

Caryatids. Supporting the 20-foot-high ceiling are four pillars of white marble, each shaped in the likeness of a stoic female elf in flowing robes with a flowering branch clutched to her chest. The caryatids face inward.

Modron. Circling the caryatids counterclockwise is a quadrone with its bow at the ready, clicking and clacking as it walks.

Bas-Reliefs. The walls are carved with bas-reliefs of unicorns and bare-branched trees.

Touching a pillar while speaking the command phrase ("Elf magic!") causes a pale light to fill the area between the four pillars, ending the following conditions on a creature in that area: blinded, deafened, petrified, poisoned, and stunned. This effect can be activated four times (once per pillar). The pillars regain their magic at the next dawn. If even one caryatid is destroyed or toppled, they all lose their magic (each has AC 17, 90 hp, immunity to poison and psychic damage; DC 30 Strength check to topple).

Halastron (the Quadrone)
Halaster found this rogue modron and named it Halastron. Its orders: remember its name; defend itself; attack anything that tries to damage the caryatids; walk counterclockwise around them otherwise.

The quadrone speaks and understands only the Modron language. It knows the caryatids can restore petrified creatures and has heard the command phrase. Its instructions don't prevent it from communicating with strangers. It disintegrates if reduced to 0 hit points.

25b–25r. Lifelike Statues

Stored here are creatures that have been turned to stone by Halaster. Each can be restored to flesh with a greater restoration spell or the magic of the caryatids in area 25a. A restored creature acts in accordance with its nature.

Area 25h is hidden behind a secret door and contains a lifelike statue of Halaster himself. Restoring it triggers a magic mouth that says, "Fools!"

Area 25m. An illusory wall behind the petrified mind flayer conceals a tunnel beyond (no substance—creatures pass right through it).

Area 25r. The petrified wererat (Zarn Kassifax) is not part of Rizzeryl's gang but tries to join it if given the chance.

26. Ancient Elven Tomb

These chambers are much older than the dwarf-hewn chambers that surround them, as evidenced by the crumbling walls, slanted floors, and thin streams of dust pouring down through cracks in the sagging ceiling. Halaster converted this tomb for the elven dead into a series of test chambers that now lie abandoned.

26a. Do Not Feed the Owlbears!

Owlbears. Two owlbears left here by Halaster are hungry and attack anything that enters this room.

Sarcophagi. A dozen elven sarcophagi arranged about this dusty room have been reduced to rubble.

Carvings. Time and neglect have all but destroyed wall carvings that depict elves mounted on elk, parading through a forest as the seasons change around them.

Secret Door. A secret door in the north wall leads to area 26b.

Treasure. Characters who search the room find a 10-pound clay pot lying amid rubble near the south wall, painted with images of a bare-footed elf druid leading a march of small, uprooted shrubs walking of their own accord. It is a pot of awakening (common wondrous item). Plant an ordinary shrub in the pot and let it grow for 30 days; it transforms into an awakened shrub friendly toward whoever planted it. When the shrub awakens, its roots break the pot, destroying it.

26b. Secret Tomb

In the middle of the dusty tomb rests a 7,500-pound alabaster sarcophagus atop a 1-foot-high block of granite. The lid is carved in the likeness of a regal elf of indeterminate age and gender, with a yawning cat stretching on the elf's chest.

A character who inspects the sarcophagus closely and succeeds on a DC 17 Wisdom (Perception) check realizes it's a solid block of alabaster with a fake lid carved to appear openable—a false tomb built to confound would-be treasure-hunters.

26c. Halaster's Crushing Eye

Destroyed Tables. Three stone tables lie strewn about the room, surrounded by ceramic shards that were once funerary urns (the owlbears in area 26a are responsible).

Rolling Sphere Trap. A 10-foot-wide hallway to the south slopes gently upward to area 26d and contains a rolling sphere trap. A 10-foot-square pressure plate (marked X on the map) triggers when 20+ pounds depress it, releasing a 10-foot-diameter stone sphere painted to look like a bloodshot eyeball. DC 20 Wisdom (Perception) to spot the plate. Wedging four or more iron spikes under the plate prevents the trap from activating.

When the sphere is released, all creatures present roll initiative; the sphere rolls with a +8 bonus. It moves in a straight line, following the sloped tunnel until it comes to rest in area 26c. Creatures caught in its path must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 55 (10d10) bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. A creature within 5 feet can use an action to attempt a DC 20 Strength check, reducing the sphere's speed by 15 feet on a success.

26d. Bloodletting Chamber

This 10-foot-high room holds a row of six slanted stone tables intended for ritualistic bloodletting. Each table is fitted with rusty shackles and leather straps. Under the lower end of each table, set into the floor, is a shallow circular stone basin for collecting blood.

26e. Abandoned Study

Makeshift Desk. The lid of a stone sarcophagus rests atop two misshapen blocks of stone at the south end of the room. Behind it is a high-backed chair of charred wood with one armrest missing.

Wall Niches. Beyond the desk, dusty books are crammed into niches in the south wall. Similar niches in the other walls contain seventeen tall alabaster funerary urns covered with dust and cobwebs.

Treasure. The alabaster urns are cracked, chipped, and worthless—containing only dust, with one exception: buried in the dust inside one is a tiny white pouch containing dust of disappearance.

The books cover alchemy, dwarven and elven history, engineering, gemcraft, the study of monstrosities, and weather prediction. All are falling apart. Among them is a badly burned wizard's spellbook that still contains: comprehend languages, crown of madness, darkvision, gaseous form, ray of sickness, remove curse, Tenser's floating disk, and unseen servant.

Aftermath

If they're weakened but not eradicated, the goblins begin constructing traps around the perimeter of their market to better protect themselves—collapsing roofs, falling nets, and poison darts. The goblins also train and deploy giant rats as watchdogs. Some of these might be Rizzeryl's wererats in rat form, sent to spy on adventurers who find their way to the goblin bazaar.

Destroying the two Xanathar Guild outposts allows the goblins to expand their territory. Unless the goblins are also wiped out, goblin patrols become more common throughout this level of the dungeon.
