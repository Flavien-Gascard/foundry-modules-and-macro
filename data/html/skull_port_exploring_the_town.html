Exploring Skullport

All location descriptions for the town of Skullport are keyed to maps 25 and 26. The town's key features are summarized below:

  • Everything has a deteriorated, haphazard look to it. Tightly packed buildings of wood and stone are piled atop one another, creating three levels: a lower level (often called the street level), a middle level, and an upper level.
  • Unnamed thoroughfares wind through the town at street level. Above this labyrinth run mazes of catwalks made from the wood of old shipwrecks. Old rigging is used to suspend the creaky catwalks.
  • The 90-foot-high cavern ceiling is covered with a forest of stalactites.
  • Most of the lanterns that once illuminated Skullport are gone, turned to rust or stolen by thieves long ago. Denizens carry their own light sources or rely on darkvision to see. Flameskulls are often mistaken from a distance for bobbing torches as they drift through town.

The town has no general store, and some merchandise abundant in Waterdeep simply isn't available here. Prices in Skullport are five times higher than normal. If an NPC is mentioned by name with no pointer to a stat block, assume the NPC is a commoner.

11. Old Markets

These open-air spaces are dreary and forlorn.

11a. Old Fish Market

Closed shops and warehouses enclose an abandoned market dotted with empty fish stands.

11b. Old Slave Market

The shops that surround this abandoned slave market appear vacant. Further investigation reveals that one shop (area 12) is open for business.

12. Dead Man's Corner

A sea hag named Olive Stillwater sells human zombies out of this dilapidated shop. She appears as an old woman covered with snails and barnacles.

Olive keeps a dozen human zombies in her cramped shop. "They're great for setting off traps!" she says with a chuckle. As payment for each zombie, she demands a tiny vial of the buyer's blood and three hairs plucked from the buyer's head. She owns a pair of rusty iron shears for this purpose. After consuming this payment, Olive gains the innate ability to cast animate dead once per day for the next three days.

Each zombie wears a rope noose around its neck. A character who buys a zombie must lead it around by the noose; otherwise, the zombie stands still and does nothing.

13. Thimblewine's Pawnshop

A cheery rock gnome named Krystaleen runs this shop. She buys reusable goods at normal cost. There is a 10 percent chance she has any given nonmagical item in stock, sold at five times the normal cost.

14. Heralds' Meet

This town square is littered with trash. Nearly all the buildings that surround it are abandoned.

15. The Black Tankard

The proprietor of this squalid, one-story tavern is a fat, dour duergar named Droon Stonedark, who works for Xanathar. Two minotaurs stand in dark corners ready to eject unruly patrons. A spectator floats amid the rafters for additional security.

Droon sells Wyrmwizz, an alcoholic swill, for 2 sp per mug. No food and no other drinks are available.

16. Gyudd's Distillery

This dwarven distillery is a three-story stone building with a sagging roof. Gyudd produces Amberjack (a sherry, 20 gp/cask), Goat's Head Ale (10 gp/cask), and Wyrmwizz (5 gp/cask).

17. Skull Square

Empty buildings surround this square, which is festooned with iron gibbets dangling from wooden masts.

18. The Sea Chest

This shop used to sell chests and strongboxes, but is now closed. Its proprietor, Tor Grayfell, had his brain eaten by a mind flayer believed to still be at large in Skullport.

19. The Flagon and the Dragon

A step up from the Black Tankard. Proprietor Cal'al Claddani, a female half-drow, always introduces herself with: "What's your poison?" She sells Amberjack (8 sp/glass) and Goat's Head Ale (4 sp/flagon), plus rare spirits. Drop a few coins and Cal'al will happily share information about Skullport — and perhaps a secret or two.

20. Dark Harvest Market

Food harvested from the Underdark is sold here by 1d6 duergar, who employ an equal number of wererats as bodyguards.

21. The Sword and Sextant

Halfling siblings Oleander and Will buy and sell maps out of this modest shop. They are eager to procure accurate maps of Undermountain.

22. The Guts & Garters

A sullen tiefling named Quietude runs Skullport's last remaining inn and spies on guests for Xanathar. Rooms cost 5 gp per day. A secret door in the cellar (DC 15 Perception) leads to a 300-foot tunnel heading west to Xanathar's dungeon hideout.

23. The Feathered Rat

Ulvira Snowveins, a venerable half-elf Harper spy, runs this pet store. Merchandise: bat (5 cp), cat (5 sp), frog/toad (5 cp), giant fire beetle (25 gp), giant rat (10 sp), lizard (5 cp), rat (5 cp), spider (5 cp), stirge (10 sp).

24. Thaglar's Foundry

Skullport's ugly four-story stone foundry. Duergar smiths under brutal taskmaster Thaglar Xundorn fashion weapons, armor, and tools.

25. The Bat's Roost

Once a seedy tavern, now a fighting den where visitors settle differences through fisticuffs while spectators place bets.

26. The Keel Hall

This two-story festhall shut down after the Spellplague and now serves as a Zhentarim hideout led by Bosskyn Gorrb, a blind tiefling spy. Zhentarim members who prove their affiliation receive:

  • Use of the festhall as a sanctuary for as long as needed.
  • A flying snake courier to deliver a message to anyone in Waterdeep.

27. The Poisoned Quill

Owner "Tas" (Tasselgryn Velldarn) is an elderly archmage and secret friend of Halaster — who visits monthly for tea. She offers to cast teleportation circle for 50 gp per party member, sending characters to a circle in a Dock Ward orphanage. Halaster's enchantments allow her alone to cast such magic within these walls.

28. Twinbeard's Traps

Workshop of Xanathar's trapsmith Thorvin Twinbeard. He's rarely here — Xanathar keeps him busy elsewhere.

29. Sargauth's Bounty

Shield dwarf brothers Darum and Duram Ghaz sell junk salvaged from the River Sargauth. 50% chance the shop is closed while the brothers are on a salvage run.

30. The Lanternlighter's

Anderian Dusk, an old half-elf, sells lanterns. He wears a hag eye pendant given to him by the sea hags of the Sargauth River (area 10c, level 3), who pay him to wear it so they can spy on Skullport. For oil, he directs customers to the Overflowing Urn (area 33).

31. Dalagor's Fortress

A bleak three-story mansion seized by the Harpers after its former necromantic owner was destroyed in the Spellplague. Dragonborn mage Felrax watches over it. Harper members who prove their affiliation receive:

  • Use of the mansion as a sanctuary for as long as needed.
  • A tip that Tasselgryn (area 27) can teleport them out for 50 gp per person.

32. Dumathoin's Jest

Duergar Ygarra Urmbaalt appraises gemstones for a flat fee of 20% of a gem's value.

33. The Overflowing Urn

This tilting building sells flasks of oil (5 sp) and casks of pitch (2 gp). "Garryth," the owner, is actually a doppelganger who killed the real Garryth five years ago and has quietly run the business ever since.

34. Nightshade's Caress

Herbs, elixirs, poisons, and medicinal fungi fill the window and shelves, all labeled and clearly priced. The apothecary, Nightshade, is an undead creature (mummy statistics) that looks like a withered crone in a tattered black robe.

35. The Worm's Gullet

Skullport's only restaurant is inside a petrified, hollowed-out purple worm. Manager Gharz Stonedark serves whatever his kobolds can find: stirge loaf, carrion crawler soufflé, flumph stew, hot shrieker pie. House drink is Wyrmwizz. Meal + drink: 5 sp per person.

36. Tanor'thal Refuge

Carved by the drow of House Tanor'thal, this stronghold resembles an upside-down spider looming over Skullport. Its hollowed-out sections are filled with thick webs and giant spiders. Other denizens avoid it — making it an excellent refuge once cleared. The drow left nothing of value behind.
